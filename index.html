Why Hamburg?

The Gateway to the World

In 1241, the prosperous trading city of Lübeck formed an alliance with Hamburg, which eventually resulted in the League of Hanseatic Cities. Membership of this powerful commercial and defensive alliance between coastal cities in Northern Europe would be crucial to Hamburg's economic position in the world.The bloodthirsty pirate Klaus Störtebeker taught Hamburg traders to fear him and yet became a legend in the Hanseatic city. Nowadays it's probably more famous for its cabaret shows, erotic art museums and the Red Light District, where the Beatles first made their first recording during the 1960s.
